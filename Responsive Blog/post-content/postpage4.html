How to Make Egusi Soup

Introduction

Egusi Soup, also known as melon seed soup, is a staple in Nigerian and Ghanaian cuisine. Made from ground melon seeds, this rich and hearty soup is packed with flavor and nutrients. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, follow these simple steps to create a mouthwatering bowl of Egusi Soup.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • 1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cups leafy vegetables (spinach, kale, or bitter leaf), washed and chopped
  • 2-3 cups meat or fish (chicken, goat meat, or catfish)
  • 2-3 tablespoons palm oil
  • 2-3 cups water or broth
  • Salt, pepper, and other seasonings to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Egusi Base
    • Heat the palm oil in a pot over medium heat.
    • Add the chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
    • Stir in the ground egusi and cook for a few minutes, allowing it to absorb the flavors.
  2. Add the Meat or Fish
    • Add your choice of meat or fish to the pot. You can use chicken, goat meat, or catfish.
    • Season with salt, pepper, and any other preferred spices.
    • Cover and let it simmer until the meat or fish is tender.
  3. Add water or Broth
    • Pour in the water or broth, adjusting the quantity based on your desired consistency..
    • Stir well to combine all the ingredients.
  4. Simmer and Thicken
    • Reduce the heat and let the soup simmer for about 20-30 minutes.
    • As it simmers, the egusi will thicken the soup. If needed, add more water or broth.
  5. Add Leafy Vegetables
    • Add the chopped leafy vegetables (spinach, kale, or bitter leaf).
    • Stir gently and let them cook for 5-10 minutes until wilted.
  6. Adjust Seasoning
    • Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning as necessary. Add more salt or pepper if needed.
  7. Serve Hot:
    • Serve your Egusi Soup hot with fufu, pounded yam, or rice.

Tips

  • Egusi Soup pairs well with traditional African staples like pounded yam, eba, or rice.
  • Feel free to customize the soup by adding other vegetables or spices.
  • Garnish with chopped fresh herbs for an extra burst of flavor.
